Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity refers to the brain's ability to restructure it's neural connections and allows the nerve cells to adjust their formation coming from a change in their environment such as difficult circumstances. This is how we learn, develop new memories and learn new skills. Research has shown that the neural connections between different regions in the brain can reorganize themselves. In a brain-damaged individual suffering from brain damage to one part of the brain, neuroplasticity can allow a healthy section in the brain to assume control over a damaged section which they can often recover from. Neuroplasticity can be increased by getting enough magnesium, sleep, reduce stress factors, exercise and challenging yourself mentally. Such as, learning a new langugage, learning an instrument or even programming!

Growth Mindset

A scientific study was conducted, where a group of children were given a set of math puzzles. Once completed, half the children were told 'Well done! You're so smart!" while the other half were told "Well done! You tried really hard!" They were then given a second, more difficult puzzle. When faced with a more difficult problem, the kids that were congratulated for being smart would give up sooner and score lower on the test. While the other kids who were congratulated on working hard would perservre and score higher.

The way you view yourself almost entirely depends on where you will end up in life. If you don't believe you can change your habits or qualities. In the example above, the kids that were praised on being smart ended up doing worse with the harder puzzles had the fixed mindset. A growth mindset is about an important belief that any skill can be honed through deliberate practice, changing the way you view failure and become more curious. Accept that you need to put the time and effort into learning something and that nothing happens overnight.
